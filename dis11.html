The most amazing fact of the culinary scenario at Kozhikode is the delectable sweetmeat called ‘Halwa’ which is a preparation made with flour. It is fried in clarified butter or ‘ghee’ with sugar and dry fruits. This savory delight is sold all over the ‘Mitthai Theruvu’ or the Sweetmeats Street or as it is commonly and colloquially called the ‘SM’ street. The Europeans and even the Middle Eastern people docking at the Kozhikode port carried away large qualities of this delightful sweat and dished its rich, savoury taste.

In this competitive world, several professions are emerging where young generation can choose and build their carrier. The success in particular profession will reach new heights if it is the area of interest and hobby. Dance is among such career where teenagers can lay the platform of their bright career along with dance being their hobby in Kozhikode city. This city has deep roots of dance culture and several dance classes have sprung at different corner of this city. The varieties of dance promoted in the dance classes in Kozhikode are western dance, classical, folk, etc. Other than these, various folk dances in Kozhikode are thira, theeyattu, theyyam and mudiyettu. Some of the popular dances classes in Kozhikode are Spark Dance Studio and Theatre, Chavara Cultural Center and Calicut Kalakshethra. Further, most of these popular dance classes are located in the areas of Orkatteri, Paramba, Puthiyangadi, Chalappuram along with popular resident areas.
