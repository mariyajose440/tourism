Welcome to God's Own Country
Wayanad is unique in that it is situated in an elevated picturesque mountainous plateau in the Western Ghats. The vast stretches of mist - capped mountains, green meadows of valleys, white water springs, blue water lakes and wild forests express the splendid natural beauty of Wayanad.
Wayanad is known for the pure honey and the spices that are much in demand in the domestic as well as foreign market. Wayanadan Pepper is also a much-sought out item and you can buy many artefacts created by the tribes or local people here. The spices and souvenirs are available at the ethnic shops near Pookot Lake. The gooseberry (amla) kept in honey is a favourite item of the local people here and it has delightful sweetness as well as nutritious quality.
Fish is the staple food of Kerala, and when you are in Wayanad, you must certainly try your hands on the exquisite cuisine, fish Moilee. The essence of fresh coconut milk and the creamy textured gravy dipped fish with the other spices would tantalize your taste-buds at once.

    Kambara Dance is another folk dance that is common among the Adiya community of Wayanad district. The community has high respect for agriculture practices and Kambara Nritham is their way to express it. Men sing folk songs and play wind and percussion instruments including the traditional drum in order to make the dance more exhilarating. They will sing and dance around the paddy fields while the women collect new saplings from the field.
The name Wayanad has been derived from the expression 'Vayal nadu' - the village of paddy fields. In the ancient times this land was ruled by the Rajas of the Veda tribe. ... Thus Wayanad fell into the hands of British, and with it began a new turn in the history of this area.
